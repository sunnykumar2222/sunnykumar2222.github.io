Reinforcement Learning for Robotic Control

An on-site international research internship teaching robotic agents to learn optimal behavior through reward — and closing the gap between simulation and reality.

Ask me about it More about my internship

Program

Cybersol Winter Program, MASCOR

Location

FH Aachen, Germany (on-site)

Focus

Deep RL, Neural Networks, Sim-to-Real

The program

I was selected for an international knowledge-exchange program at MASCOR, FH Aachen University of Applied Sciences — training on-site in Germany alongside students from multiple countries.

What I worked on

  • Trained robotic agents using Deep Reinforcement Learning, letting them learn optimal actions through reward-based feedback instead of hard-coded rules.
  • Applied neural networks to model decision-making for robotic control tasks.
  • Explored Sim-to-Real transfer methods — techniques for taking a policy trained entirely in simulation and making it work on a real, physical robot.
  • Built grounding in cognitive robotics: how a robot perceives, decides, and acts in real-world conditions.
